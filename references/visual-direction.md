## What To Borrow

- Layered paper depth.
- Slight off-grid rotation.
- Aged postcard borders.
- Postal blue and red.
- Handwritten texture as content, not decoration.
- Faded landscape images as the postcard front.
- Quiet archive entry points.
- Date collage cards built from the day's postcard fronts.
- White torn edges, taped joins, and controlled image grids.

## What Not To Borrow

- Random large English display words.
- Dense sticker overload.
- Decorative text that does not name real product content.
- Too many competing focal points.
- Childlike couple-app symbols.
- Fake location or date text unless provided by the user.
- Treating the past collection as a normal photo gallery.

## Gentle Design Rules

- Let the postcards stay readable even when the collage is rich.
- Use fewer elements with better material treatment.
- Make motion feel like paper turning, not UI showing off.
- Keep controls small and plain.
- Use silence as part of the composition.
- Let each past-date collage read as one dated object.
